Workshop materials

Practice II: Building Scientific Networks from Web of Science

Summary:
Participants use Web of Science records to construct two network types: coauthorship networks (authors connected through joint publications) and coreference networks (papers connected through shared cited references). These networks represent the social and epistemic structure of scientific fields and serve as the foundation for subsequent analyses.

Practice III: Measuring Disciplinary Hardness and Softness

Summary:
Participants analyze the networks created in Practice II using structural and epistemic indicators, including average degree centrality, average betweenness centrality, average clustering coefficient, seed clustering coefficient, and entropy-based measures. These metrics are used to compare scientific fields and assess their disciplinary hardness, softness, and knowledge organization.
